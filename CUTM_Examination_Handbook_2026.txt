1. INTRODUCTION
Centurion University of Technology and Management (CUTM) came into existence on 24 
December 2010. As part of its aspiration to develop into an institution that exhibits 'quality' both 
in teaching and examination systems, the university strives to formulate and implement an 
evaluation system which is transparent and student friendly. The main emphasis is to assess 
the knowledge gained by a student and motivate him/her to improve upon it. 
Creation of confidence amongst students about the assessment system and timely publication 
of accurate results is essential. Continuous evaluation of students' performance has become 
norm of the day. This process aims at measuring the degree of knowledge assimilated by the 
students during a course of study without bringing excess pressure. A strict and flawless 
examination system, maintaining uniformity and consistency in assessment is the backbone 
of any evaluation system. In this regard, CUTM aims to establish an examination system in 
accordance with guidelines issued by UGC, AICTE, Sector Skill Councils and State Higher 
Education Body with the following features:
- An independent system so that it can operate without fear, favour, pressure and prejudice.
- Comprehensive and transparent principles, policies, and procedures directed towards 
achievement of its goals.
- Flexibility and adaptability to the changing needs
- Team with appropriate qualifications and qualities like self-discipline, accuracy, secrecy, 
time consciousness, high degree of integrity regarding the objectives of the system and 
complete understanding of their duties and responsibilities.
- Facilities to process enormous quantities of data and generation of pre-defined documents 
with least human interaction.
The purpose of this document is to explain the features of the University's assessment system 
that will guide all examinations in the future. Students must make sure they familiarize themselves 
with this information and, if in doubt, may seek advice from their respective examination cells.

is conducted for the following types of subjects:
- Theory

- Project

- Practice + Project
- Workshop / seminar / internship

the duration of the program.

2.2 END SEMESTER EXAMINATIONS
Conducted by University both in paper setting and evaluation per Academic Calendar. 
Examination window of 4-5 weeks at end of each semester.

a. Question Paper
- Question paper pattern in Annexure-I.
- Question Paper Setter panel approved by Academic Council; identities confidential, kept by 
Director (E&QA), who has full authority to choose a QP setter.
- Balanced mix of internal and external faculty as question paper setter (Annexure-II for eligibility).

b. Eligibility to Appear for Examinations
- Subject-wise registration required before semester commencement.
- Minimum attendance requirement for appearing in examinations in all programmes is 75%, 
calculated per subject (NOT aggregate). Includes class work, practice work, project work 
(including internships) in both online/physical modes. Same rule applies for internal exams.
- Condoning of minimum attendance (medical/other reasons, allowed by Deans) limited to max 10%.
- Students debarred from main semester exams due to attendance shortage can appear for 
Examination on Demand (EOD) if they have 50% or more attendance in the subject.
- Students with less than 50% attendance are not removed from academic record; such subjects 
awarded "R" grade at result publication.
- Students must register such subjects by 30 April, registration data shared with QA cell by 10 May.
- A candidate is allowed in semester exam only after being issued an Admit Card. If lost, can be 
re-obtained from campus exam cell on payment of fee.
- Malpractice → 'M' grade in that subject + possible disciplinary action; can appear only in 
subsequent exams per university decision.
- Absent in registered subject → 'S' grade, can appear in subsequent EODs.

c. Conduct of Examinations
QA cell conducts End-Term Examination at end of each semester per Academic Calendar window:
- Prepares and announces examination schedule based on registration data.
- Appoints officials/staff for conduct of exam at respective campuses.
- Issues instructions for flawless, unbiased conduct minimizing unfair means.
- Prepares, prints, packs, dispatches question papers to constituent campuses.
(Details in Annexure-III, IV, V)

d. Evaluation
Evaluation systems (any combination, as decided by competent authority):
- Centralized Evaluation: at QA cell under Evaluation Coordinator.
- Delegated Evaluation: Answer Books issued to teachers/persons engaged by competent 
authority; evaluated books + marks returned within stipulated time.
- Any other system decided by University.

3. GRADING SYSTEM
CUTM follows "Absolute" grading system. Ten Point grading system on base of 10.

Qualification | Grade | Score on 100 Percentage Points | Point
Outstanding | 'O' | 90 and above up to 100 | 10
Excellent | 'E' | 80 and above but less than 90 | 9
Very Good | 'A' | 70 and above but less than 80 | 8
Good | 'B' | 60 and above but less than 70 | 7
Fair | 'C' | 50 and above but less than 60 | 6
Pass | 'D' | 40 and above but less than 50 | 5
Failed | 'F' | Below 40 | 2
Malpractice | 'M' | --- | 0
Absent | 'S' | --- | 0
Re-registration | 'R' | --- | 0
Improvement | 'I' | --- | 0

N.B. Grade C shall be considered as average, Grade D shall be pass Grade for theory and 
Grade C shall be Pass Grade for Practical/Project/Workshop mode paper.
BSc(AG), MSc(AG), BSc(Fishery Science) follow ICAR norms; MPharm, BPharm, DPharm follow 
PCI norms; BVSc & AH follow VCI regulations; B.Sc(Nursing) follows nursing regulations.

GPA for mixed courses (T-P-P mode) = weighted average of individual marks weighted by 
credit of the component:
GPA = (Wt*marks of theory + Wp*marks of practice + Wpr*marks of project) / (Wt+Wp+Wpr)
where W = credit weight. All components individually evaluated for 100 marks.

Example: Course "Building of Cloud Infrastructure" (Theory+Practice+Project, 1-2-2 weight). 
Student scores 87 (theory), 89 (practice), 92 (project) out of 100:
Wt*Theory = 1*87=87, Wp*Practical = 2*89=178, Wpr*Project = 2*92=184
Sum = 449, Total credit = 5, Result = 449/5 = 89.8, rounded to 90 → Grade 'O'

SGPA / GPA = CREDIT INDEX / Sum of CREDITS for a Semester
CGPA / OGPA = CREDIT INDEX of all previous Semester(s) / CREDITS of all Previous Semester(s)
Percentage of marks = CGPA x 10 or OGPA x 10

4. RESULT PUBLICATION
- Coded results placed before approving authority by Director (E&QA) for approval. 
- Approved results declared after decoding, sent to respective colleges for display to students.

5. GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL SYSTEM
- Students contact respective campus examination cells for grievances related to examination.
- Campus exam cells, after verification, may forward to QA cell if required.
- Records of grievances and resolution timeframe maintained at campus exam cell.
- Refer Annexure-XI for key examination activities timeline.

Rechecking of Answer-books
- Students may apply for revaluation/photocopy of theory answer books through college exam-cells. 
Exam-cells forward consolidated list within 10 days of result publication (CUTM/EXAM/EX-005).
- Examinees found guilty of malpractice/misconduct are not eligible to apply for verification/
revaluation/photocopy.
- Verification of internal, Practical/lab and project/workshop marks not permitted.
- Revaluation results are binding on the candidate.
- Revised marks, if only higher, considered for amendment of results.
- A separate examiner re-examines the answer book and submits question-wise rechecked mark-foil.
- Corrected statement processed; revised result intimated to colleges.

Issue of Photocopy of Answer Book
- Provided through campus examination cells; no photocopies of blank pages provided.
- Made available after necessary corrections (if revaluation done); examiner/moderator identity 
concealed before photocopying.
- Student is sole custodian of photocopies; must not transfer or misuse them. CUTM acts as 
deemed fit against misuse.

6. PROVISION TO CLEAR BACKLOGS
a. Examination On Demand (EOD)
University conducts two EODs per semester + one extended EOD during summer, for students 
to clear backlog papers by paying required fees. Students with theory component backlogs may 
appear for EODs and choose subjects of their choice. 
Pass-out batch students can opt for 100%-mark option in external component(s); internal mark 
nullified, evaluation done taking external component as 100%. Available till studentship valid 
(twice the duration of the course).

b. Laboratory/Sessional Backlogs
- Students with backlog in practice/project/workshop mode papers may appear for "Laboratory 
Backlogs Examinations" by paying required fees, scheduled once a year during summer vacation.
- Entire laboratory/sessional repeated in fast-track mode; evaluation for 100% of components 
(both internal and external).

Credit Shortfall / Re-registration of Subjects
- Students with attendance/credit shortfall in a semester can re-register and repeat the course 
when next offered, along with regular students, by paying prescribed fee.
- Passing-out batch students with credit shortfall for degree award must reregister subjects, 
attend coursework, undergo all assessment procedures in subsequent semester. University 
discourages this; students advised to complete credit requirements within course duration.

7. EXCEPTIONAL CASES
a. Special Examination 
Provision for passing-out students with backlogs (not more than two theory papers) who need 
their degree urgently (job/higher studies), by depositing prescribed fee. QA cell conducts exam; 
result published within seven working days of valid application receipt. Student appeals to 
concerned Dean with proof; Dean may recommend to QA. University discourages such practice 
except in exceptional circumstances with valid reasons and due approval.

8. PROVISION FOR SKIPPING SUBJECT(S)
Under CBCS, students may skip paper(s) if they fulfill basket-wise minimum credit requirements 
per Academic Regulation for their batch (Refer Annexure-IX/XIII for skip norms under CBCS).

9. AWARD OF DEGREE AND CONVOCATION
a. Award of Degree
Eligible for award of degree if:
- Fulfilled Degree requirements 
- No dues to Institute, Departments, Hostels, Library 
- No disciplinary action pending

b. Merit Certificate
Eligibility:
- Highest CGPA in respective branch/programme
- At least 8.00 CGPA, all papers cleared in first attempt
- Branch/programme strength not less than ten students

c. Gold Medal 
Criteria:
- Total students in programme not less than 50
- Minimum 8.00 CGPA, highest CGPA in the programme
- No academic-ground disciplinary action during study period
- Cleared all courses in first attempt

d. Convocation
- Held once a year as Visitor directs on Management recommendation.
- Director (E&QA) prepares eligible candidates list post-results for the annual Convocation.
- Missed eligible names can be added later with VC's prior approval.
- Degree Certificate distribution at Convocation or any time after, but no candidate admitted to 
convocation without prior registration and fee payment.
- Successful backlog-exam students get provisional certificate from Director (E&QA); entitled to 
final Degree Certificate at next annual Convocation.

10. NATIONAL ACADEMIC DEPOSITORY (NAD)
National System by MHRD and UGC via Digilocker, for institutions to digitally issue academic 
awards directly to students' online NAD accounts. Benefits: immediate availability, permanent 
online record, no risk of loss/damage, anytime access, verified records sharable with 
employers/banks without photocopies, assists admission/enrollment. CUTM uploads academic 
documents to NAD since 2018 graduating batch; students must register in NAD themselves 
(process in Annexure-XI/XVI).

11. ADDITIONAL TRANSCRIPT
Students submit application + supporting documents to college exam cell with prescribed fee for 
additional transcript copies (CUTM/EXAM/EX-006).

12. ANSWER PAPER RETAINED IN SAFE CUSTODY
- Answer books made into subject-wise packets, stacked in racks with proper ventilation, free 
from termites/pests.
- Each answer-book's removal/return recorded in computer with purpose, by in-charge of storage.
- Storage area fumigated and aired periodically.

13. DESTRUCTION OF ANSWER BOOK
- Unless specific VC instruction, all evaluated answer books destroyed after five years of result 
declaration.
- Certificate given by Dy. Director (E&QA) detailing destroyed answer books, retained by QA cell.

ANNEXURE-I: QUESTION PAPER PATTERN
Question paper: 100 marks, three parts, three hours' duration.
Part A: 10 short questions (2 marks each) = 20 marks
Part B: 5 Long questions (12 marks each, may have sub-questions e.g. 6+6, 6+5+1, 6+4+2, 
6+3+3, 5+4+3, 4+4+4) = 60 marks
Part C: 4 Short notes (5 marks each) = 20 marks

Guidelines:
- Questions within syllabus scope, proper module-wise distribution.
- Aligned with Course Outcomes (COs).
- Degree of difficulty: 50% straight questions, 20% moderate level, 30% difficult level.
- If graph paper/calculator/reference material allowed, mention in remark column.
- Script should be clear and legible.
Note: BSc(AG)/MSc(AG)/BFSc follow ICAR guidelines; MPharm/BPharm/DPharm follow PCI; 
BVSc & AH follow VCI; B.Sc(Nursing) follows nursing regulations.

ANNEXURE-II: ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA FOR QUESTION PAPER SETTERS AND EVALUATORS
Criteria for selecting QP setters/evaluators:
- PhD/M.Tech degree
- Subject specialization
- Minimum 5 years industry and teaching experience
- Recommendation of HOD/DEAN/Director
- Previous experience in QP setting/evaluation
Additional factors: moderator feedback, student complaint, time schedule commitment.
Internal faculties for QP setting/evaluation should not exceed 50% of total.

ANNEXURE-III: CONDUCT OF SEMESTER EXAMINATION

Preparation of the Room
Administrative staff sets up venue, ensures availability of: Answer books, Attendance/Signature 
sheets, Graph paper (if relevant), Venue Information (Centre Superintendent/Exam Office phone), 
Statistical tables (if relevant). Invigilator ensures appropriate pre-exam conditions. Administrative 
Departments informed in advance to avoid maintenance disturbance during exam period.

Requirements/Guidelines for Examination Hall
- Roll list sent with examination material.
- Only listed students with University Registration Card + Admit Card allowed.
- Number of candidates per room decided by college based on room size, to prevent cheating.
- Seating per ERP-generated seating plan.
- Registration number pasted/indicated on respective desk.
- One invigilator per 20 students, must be a registered University teacher.
- Invigilators remain in hall for entire exam period, advised to move around.
- 10% reliever invigilators per college for temporary absence cases.

Seating Arrangements
Only one student per bench, minimum one-meter gap between students. A room for 60 students 
accommodates only 20 during exams. Roll number written per attendance sheet on each bench. 
Adequate space between benches; benches/chairs cleaned daily. Rooms kept locked, opened 
only 15 minutes before exam commencement. Consolidated room-wise seating arrangement 
displayed at prominent places.

Gate Checking
Special team for gate checking. Items NOT allowed: cell phones, programmable calculator, any 
printed/handwritten material except Registration Card, I-Card, Admit Card, calculator, writing/
drawing materials.

Reporting for Invigilation Duty
Senior Invigilators report to Examinations Office 30 minutes prior to collect: Question paper 
(sealed envelope), Charts/tables, Attendance sheet, Seating plan, Temporary Absence Forms.

Admitting the Candidates 
Invigilator authorizes admittance 10 minutes before published start time (size-dependent). 
Announces rules and conduct; ensures only authorized materials permitted; draws attention to 
official clock.

Starting the Examination
Starts at mentioned time; any delay added to finishing time.

Candidates Arriving Late
No entry after 15 minutes of exam start. Late arrivals (within 15 min) get no extra time at end.

Supervision
Invigilators positioned for clear view of all candidates, remain for entire duration. Reliever 
invigilators deployed for occasional absences; room never left without an invigilator.

ID Checks
Students confirm identity via University Registration No. card and Admit card, placed on desk for 
checking. Students without documents report to exam office, get signed slip (CUTM/EXAM/EX-001), 
valid for one exam only.

Confirming Attendance
Taken 30 minutes after exam start. Each signature sheet page has 20 students' names/Reg numbers. 
Invigilators check University Registration Card + admit card before marking attendance. Absentees 
marked "ABSENT" in capital red ink. Present/absent counts recorded and signed by invigilator(s).

Suspected Irregularity or Misconduct 
Invigilator informs supervisor; Centre Superintendent's directive is final. Written report submitted 
to exam cell post-exam (CUTM/EXAM/EX-004). Illicit material confiscated, script annotated.

Problems with Question Papers
Points requiring clarification notified immediately to exam cell; invigilators do not advise on 
meaning/interpretation of questions.

Additional Paper
No additional sheets provided normally; exceptional cases allow one more answer-book with 
Centre Superintendent's concurrence, endorsed in attendance sheet.

Leaving Early
No candidate leaves before one hour has elapsed (without special permission), nor leaves desk 
during last 15 minutes except to submit paper (CUTM/EXAM/EX-003). One-hour exams: candidates 
wait for invigilator's permission.

Toilet Arrangements
Toilets cleaned, opened only 10 minutes before exam start. Checked after each break for written 
material.

Drinking water
Available in each room; attendant may ensure availability.

Medical Facilities
Arranged by college administration to avoid mishaps.

Ending the Examination
Invigilator announces 1 hour remaining, then 15 minutes remaining. Ends at said time unless extra 
time added for delay. Candidates remain seated and silent until scripts collected.

Collection of Completed Scripts
All answer books collected before students leave hall; no taking answer books/paper out. Question 
papers may be kept unless specified otherwise. Graphs attached with tag. Invigilator counts books 
to tally with appeared students.

Handing over the answer-scripts to examination cell
Invigilators hand over: Answer books (subject-wise, attendance sheet serial order), Attendance 
sheet, Spare question paper copies, Other exam material.
Collection, Packing and Forwarding: Centre Superintendent ensures answer books matched with 
signature sheet, packed and sealed immediately post-exam. Attendance sheets packed separately, 
sealed. Attendance statement countersigned, submitted separately at dispatch (mandatory).

ANNEXURE-IV: ROLE OF EXAMINATION CONDUCTING AUTHORITIES

A. Centre Superintendent
- Maintains confidentiality and discipline at Exam Centers, ensures smooth conduct.
- Answer Booklets/Question Papers kept under lock and key in safe custody until distribution.
- Question Paper packets opened only 30 minutes before exam time, in CS presence.
- May allow candidate to appear without name in attendance sheet after taking undertaking.
- Ensures proper gate checking at commencement of each sitting.
- Shortage of question papers: arranges additional copies, reports to Director E&QA immediately, 
with care to avoid malpractice.
- Visits each hall during exam to ensure invigilator vigilance.
- Malpractice cases: ensures documentation per handbook guidelines.
- Necessary security arrangements at Exam Centre.
- Ensures answer book collection/dispatch per guidelines: arranged by subject/exam-wise, 
bundled/sealed in CS presence, dispatched same day to QA unless instructed otherwise.

B. External Observer
- Senior faculty/reputed person nominated by Director (E&QA) to observe semester exam conduct.
- Reports to college exam cell one day prior to commencement, ensures arrangements adequate.
- Written report to Director (E&QA) on security of question papers/answer-books.
- Reports unfair means/practices immediately to Dy. Director (E&QA); does not leave centre 
during exam period.
- Final written report after exam cycle/engagement complete, in sealed envelope to in-charge 
exam cell for dispatch to Director, E&QA.

C. Internal Supervisor
- Senior faculty nominated to observe semester exam conduct.
- Monitors invigilators performing duties properly.
- Vigilant during exam, ensures rules/regulations implemented appropriately.
- Oversees appropriate facilities maintained, fair and impartial conduct.

D. Flying Squad
- Assigned to internal or external staff.
- Overviews/supervises exam conduct, surprise inspections to eliminate unfair means, maintains 
exam credibility.
- Consists of two or three members.
- Submits report after engagement, handed to in-charge exam cell for dispatch to Director, E&QA.

E. Invigilators
- Examination duty compulsory; alternative arrangement in emergency.
- Report to exam section half hour before exam to receive materials.
- Enter hall 15 minutes before scheduled time.
- Implement code of conduct for students.
- Ensure no unauthorized material carried.
- Check students occupy seats per seating plan.
- No candidate admitted without admit card; answer script given only to those with proper admit card.
- Distribute answer books 10 minutes before exam, ask students to fill details.
- Verify signature/registration number/subject/date on cover page, sign in space provided.
- Distribute question paper 5 minutes before exam.
- Remain VIGILANT throughout, move in hall to prevent indiscipline/copying.
- Should not help candidates explain questions or give hints.
- Take attendance after checking admit card.
